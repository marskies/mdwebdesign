# Marina DiPonio Web Design — Brand Guidelines

_A quick reference to help with logo design. These guidelines describe the existing look, feel, and voice of the brand so a new logo fits naturally alongside everything else._

---

## 1. The Brand at a Glance

- **Name:** Marina DiPonio Web Design
- **What it is:** A solo web designer creating thoughtful websites for small businesses and personal portfolios.
- **Tagline:** *Websites that tell your story*
- **Supporting line:** *Thoughtful web design for small businesses and personal portfolios.*

**The promise behind the brand:** fast turnaround, honest and upfront pricing, and a personal touch built for small businesses and individuals (not big corporations).

---

## 2. Personality & Tone

The brand should feel **friendly, reassuring, approachable, and quietly professional**. It is warm and human, not corporate or flashy. Think "a trusted, talented friend who happens to be great at building websites."

**Words that describe the vibe:** approachable, polished, calm, creative, dependable, personal, modern.

**Avoid:** anything cold, overly techy, aggressive, or generic-agency. No clip-art, no heavy gradients-for-the-sake-of-it, no busy or cluttered marks.

---

## 3. Color Palette

The signature look is a soft trio of muted, natural tones that appear together as a gradient across the site. These three are the heart of the brand:

| Role | Name | Hex |
|------|------|-----|
| Primary accent | Warm gold | `#C4A882` |
| Secondary accent | Dusty blue | `#7BA3B3` |
| Tertiary accent | Sage green | `#9BB87A` |

These often flow left-to-right as a gentle gradient (gold to blue to green). A logo can use one of these, all three, or the gradient — whatever reads cleanly at small sizes.

**Neutrals used throughout:**

| Role | Hex |
|------|-----|
| Near-black text | `#1A1A1A` |
| Muted text | `#666` / `#888` |
| White background | `#FFFFFF` |
| Off-white background | `#FAFAFA` |

**Tip for the logo:** It should look great in a single flat color (e.g. the near-black `#1A1A1A` or the warm gold `#C4A882`) as well as in the full three-color gradient version. Please provide both.

---

## 4. Typography

The site uses three typefaces, each with a clear job:

- **Outfit** — headings and titles. Geometric, clean, modern, friendly.
- **DM Sans** — body text. Highly readable, neutral, warm.
- **Kalam** — occasional handwritten accents (little notes near the hero). Casual and personable; used sparingly.

If the logo includes a wordmark, **Outfit** is the natural starting point to keep it consistent with the headings, though a custom or lightly customized letterform is welcome. The handwritten Kalam style could inspire a small personal accent (but shouldn't dominate).

---

## 5. What the Logo Needs to Do

- Work as the site header mark (currently a simple wordmark, `logo.png`).
- Stay crisp and legible at small sizes (think browser tab, mobile header, social avatar).
- Read clearly in **one solid color** and in the **three-color gradient**.
- Sit comfortably on both white (`#FFFFFF`) and off-white (`#FAFAFA`) backgrounds.
- Feel handmade and personal rather than mass-produced.

---

## 6. Deliverables Wishlist

- Primary logo (gradient version) + single-color version
- A compact icon/monogram version (e.g. "MD" or "M") for tight spaces and avatars
- Light-background and dark-background variants
- Vector source files (SVG) plus high-res PNGs with transparent backgrounds
- A note on minimum size and clear-space so it's always used correctly
